Cuppa Lumpia Signage Plan

Every sign, in the order a customer meets it: awareness out on the road → directional arrows near the turn → sandwich boards at the driveway → the full sign at the stand → an order-ahead card at the register that turns the visit into the next scan. One gold look the whole way, so the trail feels connected.

MapPlacementthe three drop points, by distance to the Lovgreen turn

The real run: Madison Ave is the arterial, so drivers come from the north and south, so The Hook goes ≈¼ mile out in each direction, The Action ~350 ft before the Madison & Lovgreen turn, then it’s 0.2 mi west on Lovgreen to the driveway. From 305: eastbound drivers turn onto Lovgreen and head straight to you (no second turn) for 0.3 mi. Put a Hook right at the 305 corner facing east, then the same Arrival at the driveway. Confirm on-site: walk the actual footage, and print directional art as explicit Side A (northbound approach) and Side B (southbound approach) panels. Don’t flip one piece of art, or a left arrow reads as a right when reversed.

Sign 1The Hook≈ ¼ mile ahead: keyword + “ahead”; cues drivers to ease off the gas
The goal

Buy reaction time. At highway speed a driver needs roughly a quarter-mile to read a word, register the craving, and ease off the gas. So this sign says one thing: the product and the distance, and nothing else. No menu, no logo fine print. Its only job is to plant “lumpia, soon” so the next sign can close it.

How to display
  • 48 × 36″ coroplast on an H-stake or twin steel stakes, driven into the shoulder.
  • Angle it 10–15° toward oncoming traffic, not flat to the road, so it faces the windshield, not the passing door.
  • Print Side A and Side B as separate panels, one per approach direction.
Sign 2The Action300–400 ft before the turn: explicit street name + arrow to signal & merge
The goal

Convert the primed driver into a turn. This is the commitment point: an explicit street name and a single, unmistakable arrow. Hesitation here means a missed turn, so it answers where early enough for them to signal and slow before the merge.

How to display
  • Same 48 × 36″ coroplast, placed ~350 ft before the Madison & Lovgreen turn.
  • Arrow direction is everything, so mount two single-sided panels back-to-back, never one flipped file, so left/right reads correctly from each direction.
Sign 3The Arrivalat the entrance/driveway — full logo, welcome & details (sandwich boards + gold feather flag)
The goal

Mark the doorway and answer the last three questions: open? what? when? The feather flag is the beacon: its vertical motion is the one thing that reliably reads “pull in HERE” to a driver scanning a rural shoulder. The A-frame then delivers the welcome and the proof.

How to display
  • 24 × 36″ A-frame set parallel to the shoulder at the driveway mouth, two-sided by nature, so it works both directions.
  • Pair it with a 2.5 × 11 ft gold feather flag. The flag catches the eye from down the road; the board closes the decision at the turn-in.
At standThe Menuthe experience continues: order-ahead QR
The goal

Prime the line and speed every transaction. By the time a guest reaches the counter they should already know their order. So the board leads with a clean item → quantity → price hierarchy in type readable down the whole line, so fewer questions at the window, faster turns, a calmer counter.

How to display: three options
  • Tabletop easel (wrought-iron or wide-base wood) on the counter, lifting the 24″ width ~6″ into direct line of sight and protected from foot traffic.
  • Canopy-frame mount: zip-tie or clamp through the grommets to the upper bars or front leg of a 10 × 10 ft pop-up. Off the table, just above eye level, readable from 15 ft, and windproof.
  • Top-rider or small stand 5 ft ahead of the counter, intercepting the waiting line and primes orders before guests reach you.

Print matte or satin, never gloss. Morning and afternoon sun on a glossy panel blows the prices out from key angles. A flat finish stays legible in any light.

At counterOrder-Ahead Cardstabletop 5 × 7″ by the register: one card per ask, swap by season
The goal

Turn this visit into the next one. Payment is the highest-intent moment a guest will ever have with you, so capture it: a re-order, a drop-list signup, a frozen-dozen or catering lead. One ask per card, swapped to match the season: Order Ahead as the everyday default, Drop List into a sell-out weekend, Frozen for the off-season, Menu & Hours at a pop-up or new spot.

How to display
  • 5 × 7″ (or 4 × 6″) in an acrylic slant-back stand at the register, beside the card reader.
  • Keep it clear of the menu board so the QR is the single obvious action at the point of sale: one scan, one job.
  • QR and the cuppalumpia.com baseline both resolve to the site, so a fumbled scan still finds you.

Production: all gold-forward to match. For the two directional signs, print separate single-sided panels mounted back-to-back (Side A northbound / Side B southbound) rather than one flipped double-sided file. This guarantees each arrow points correctly and avoids reversed-arrow errors. Sandwich boards are two-sided by nature. The stand sign carries the QR; road signs use the URL only.

Sizes: Hook & Action road panels 48 × 36″ (coroplast on an H-frame or two stakes); Arrival A-frames 24 × 36″ per side; gold feather flag ≈ 2.5 × 11 ft at the driveway; stand menu sign 24 × 18″. Same big type and gold field across all road panels so they read as one trail.
